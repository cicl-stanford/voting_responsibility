In this example, the vote was about policy #123187 which was supported by the Republican party. 4 politicians were in the committee. In order for the policy to be passed, at least 2 committee members were required to vote for it.

John, a Republican, voted for the policy. So did Bill, also a Republican. Steve, a Democrat, didn't vote for the policy. Robert, another Democrat, voted in favor of the policy.

Since 3 out of 4 committee members voted for the policy and 2 votes were required for the policy to pass, the policy was accepted.

Each of the committee members knows the following information before they make a decision whether to vote for the policy:

  1. the number of people in the committee
  2. the minimum number of votes required for the policy to pass
  3. each politician's political affiliation
  4. whether the policy is supported by the Democratic or Republican party

In the experiment, you will judge to what extent different committee members are responsible for whether or not a given policy was passed.
